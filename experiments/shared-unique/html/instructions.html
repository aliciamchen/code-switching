This study consists of two parts. In the first part, you will observe two groups of players, each group playing a communication game. In the second part, you will answer some questions based on what you have observed in the first part.

üí¨ Part 1: Communication game üí¨

In the first part of the task, you will observe two groups of players playing a communication game. The players are real participants who previously played this game in an earlier study.

Each player belongs to one of two groups: the red group or the blue group. There are four players in each group. The players interact with other members of their own group throughout the game.

üí¨ Part 1: Communication game üí¨

How does the communication game work?

Everyone playing the game sees the same set of 6 pictures, which will look something like this:

In each group, there are 4 players. In each round, one of the players in the group is assigned the Speaker role and the others are assigned the Listener role.

The Speaker sees a box secretly marking one of the pictures as the target:

The Speaker's job is to send a description of the target through the chatbox so that the Listeners are able to pick it out of the set. They can write whatever description they think will best allow the Listeners to identify the target.

After the Speaker sends a message, the Listeners read it and each click the picture they believe is the target. They are also allowed to respond by sending messages back through the chatbox until they are ready to make a selection. At that time, everyone will be given feedback: the Speaker sees which picture each of the Listeners selected, and the Listeners each see what the correct picture was.

In each round, the Speaker communicates about each of the 6 pictures. Then, the Speaker role then rotates to the next player, and the next round begins.

We will show you videos of the gameplay.

For each round and group, there will be 6 videos, one for each of the 6 pictures. Each video displays the conversation between the players. At the end of each video, you will see what the target picture was, as well as what each player selected.

The Speaker's messages will be on the left side of the chatbox and the Listeners' messages will be on the right side of the chatbox. Here is an example video:

There are a total of 6 rounds in the game. You will see the gameplay for each of the two groups before moving on to the next round. Note that the chat messages displayed in the videos are sped up to facilitate reading through them.

In the second part of the task, it will be up to you to choose words that go with these pictures.

On each screen, you will see two pictures with a word describing each, and you will have to pick one of them. We will tell you who will see your choice (a member of the red group, a member of the blue group, or a member of either group) and what question they will be answering when they see it. Your goal is to try to get them to answer the question in a certain way based on the word you select.

Please read carefully, so you know who will see the picture and words you chose, what question they will be answering, and how you want them to answer it.
