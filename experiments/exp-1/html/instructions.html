This study consists of two parts. In the first part, you will observe two groups of players, each group playing a communication game. In the second part, you will answer some questions based on what you have observed in the first part.

üí¨ Part 1: Communication game üí¨

In the first part of the task, you will observe two groups of players playing a communication game. The players are real participants who previously played this game in an earlier study.

Each player belongs to one of two groups: the red group or the blue group. There are four players in each group. The players interact with other members of their own group throughout the game.

üí¨ Part 1: Communication game üí¨

How does the communication game work?

Everyone sees the same set of 6 pictures, which look something like this:

In each group, there are 4 players. In each round, one of the players in the group is assigned the Speaker role and the others are assigned the Listener role.

The Speaker sees a box secretly marking one of the pictures as the target:

The Speaker's job is to send a description of the target through the chatbox so that the Listeners are able to pick it out of the set. They can write whatever description they think will best allow the Listeners to identify the target.

After the Speaker sends a message, the Listeners read it and each click the picture they believe is the target. They are also allowed to respond by sending messages back through the chatbox until they are ready to make a selection. At that time, everyone will be given feedback: the Speaker sees which picture each Listeners selected, and the Listeners each see whether their selection was correct or not.

In each round, the Speaker communicates about each of the 6 pictures once. Then, the Speaker role then rotates to the next player, and the next round begins.

We will show you videos of the gameplay.

For each round and group, there will be 6 videos, one for each of the 6 pictures. Each video displays the conversation between the players. At the end of each video, you will see what the target picture was, as well as what each player selected.

The speaker's messages will be on the left side of the chatbox and the listeners' messages will be on the right side of the chatbox. Here is an example video:

There are a total of 6 rounds in the game. You will see the gameplay for each of the two groups before moving on to the next round. So, for example, you'll see Round 1 for the red group, and then Round 1 for the blue group, and then Round 2 for the red group, and then Round 2 for the blue group, and so on.

Note that the chat messages displayed in the videos are sped up to facilitate reading through them! But, feel free to pause the videos and/or take your time to read through the chat messages.

In order to earn the highest possible bonus, please pay attention during this first part of the task. You will need to use what you have observed in this part, to answer the questions in the second part of the task.

ü§® Part 2: Questions ü§®

In the second part of the task, you will answer some questions based on what you observed in the first part.

There are a total of 12 questions. For each question, you will be given two answers to select from, each option a picture with its corresponding description.

The descriptions you choose will be sent to the players of the game you just observed! For each question, we will tell you (1) who will see the desciption you select; and (2) what we will prompt them to answer, based on the description you select. Your job is to choose the description that you think will help them best answer the question.

Then, after the task ends, we will send your responses and their corresponding questions to the players, for them to answer. You will earn an additional bonus, up to XXX, based on how correctly the players in the game answer the questions using your responses.
